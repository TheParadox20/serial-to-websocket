LoRa Fundamentals

A quick primer on Long Range radio — the physical layer powering low-power, wide-area wireless links.

What is LoRa?

LoRa (short for Long Range) is a proprietary radio modulation technique developed by Semtech. It uses chirp spread spectrum (CSS) to trade data rate for receiver sensitivity, allowing tiny low-power devices to communicate over kilometres while drawing only milliamps of current.

LoRa is just the physical layer. LoRaWAN is the networking layer built on top of it — defining gateways, addressing, encryption, and the duty-cycle rules that keep shared bands usable.

Core Parameters

Frequency Band 868 MHz (EU), 915 MHz (US), 433 MHz (Asia). License-free ISM bands.
Spreading Factor (SF) SF7–SF12. Higher = longer range, lower bitrate, longer airtime.
Bandwidth 125, 250, or 500 kHz. Wider = faster but less sensitive.
Coding Rate 4/5 to 4/8. Forward error correction overhead.
Transmit Power Typically +14 dBm (EU) up to +20 dBm in some regions.
Sensitivity Down to −137 dBm — below the thermal noise floor.

Range vs. Bitrate

Spreading factor is the main knob — here’s the rough tradeoff at 125 kHz bandwidth:

SF Bitrate Airtime (12-byte packet) Range (line of sight)
SF7~5.5 kbps~40 ms~2 km
SF9~1.8 kbps~165 ms~5 km
SF12~0.3 kbps~1.3 s15+ km

When to use LoRa

Good fit

Sparse telemetry — GPS trackers, soil moisture, livestock collars, utility meters. Anywhere you need a small payload (< ~200 bytes) delivered occasionally from a battery-powered device over a long distance.

Bad fit

Voice, video, firmware updates, or anything chatty. Duty-cycle limits (typically 1% on EU sub-bands) cap how much airtime a device can use per hour. LoRa is built for infrequent traffic.

Typical Stack

A LoRa node is usually a microcontroller (ESP32, STM32, Atmega) wired over SPI to a LoRa transceiver chip (SX1276, SX1262, LLCC68). The MCU controls modulation parameters, hands a payload to the radio, and goes back to sleep. On the receive side, a gateway demodulates packets and forwards them upstream — in this project, over a serial link into a WebSocket bridge.
